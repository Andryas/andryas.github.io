Let's take a walk in Montreal

May 17, 2022 in real-estates

Greetings earthlings!

Today I want to explore with you the best places to walk in Montreal (Island) according to walkscore.com. How are we going to do that? Through data from centris.ca, I crawled approximately 10k properties, where we got the walkscore and some other information for future analysis.

To start, let’s see what the walkscore.com says about their mission:

Our Mission Walk Score’s mission is to promote walkable neighbourhoods. Walkable neighbourhoods are one of the simplest and best solutions for the environment, our health, and our economy.

and their vision

Our Vision Our vision is for every property listing to read: Beds: 3 Baths: 2 Walk Score: 84. We want to make it easy for people to evaluate walkability and transportation when choosing where to live.

Basically, they try to provide a score that summarizes the accessibility of a place; with that in mind, areas, where we have bus stops, metros and taxis stops will have the best scores. And places with few of those things will have a low score.

Figure 1: Montreal neighbourhood

Figure 2: All walkscore properties collected from centris

As we can see in the plot above, places where we have a colour tending to red will have a high score, and areas with the colour tending to blue will have a low score. We notice that South-West of Montreal has lower scores; I don’t know exactly why, but probably because there are many houses there, so it sounds more like a “family place,” where it has a lower population density.

Now, imagine that you are new in the city and you don’t know anything and want to rent a condo here. You probably will try to find something downtown; it is where everything happens, right? But, downtown, in general, is more expensive than some furthest places, for that let’s try to find the best neighbourhoods according to the walkscore and find the cheapest places to rent.

Above we have a plot that we can interact with to see the average walkscore by neighbourhood, and below we have the boxplot of which neighbourhood to have an idea of how the walkscore is distributed by neighbourhood.

The top neighbourdhoods are:

  • Le Plateau-Mont-Royal
  • Ville-Marie
  • Rosemont-La Petite-Patrie
  • Villeray-Saint-Michel-Parc-Extension
  • Le Sud-Ouest
  • Westmount
  • Côte-des-Neiges-Notre-Dame-de-Grâce
  • Saint-Léonard
  • Outremont
  • Mont-Royal
  • Mercier-Hochelaga-Maisonneuve
